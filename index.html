Operating principles

  1. Start with the real workflow, under real pressure.
    A customer buying an add-on, a business owner managing call forwarding, or a coach building a lineup mid-inning — the product has to hold up in the moment that matters.
  2. Build reusable systems, not one-off features.
    Catalog flags, treatment matrices, consent components, analytics taxonomies, and role permissions create compounding leverage across journeys.
  3. Move teams from ad hoc to governed — without slowing them down.
    Turn scattered intake and reactive work into clear lanes, shared expectations, and measurable outcomes that teams actually adopt.
  4. Pair business judgment with builder fluency.
    I don't need to be the engineer to reason about APIs, dependencies, tradeoffs, and edge cases — and increasingly, I build to stay close to the craft.
